The African Company was a series of battles in World War II fought in North Africa between the Axis, represented mainly by Italian military formations, and the Allies, which included British, Australian, South African, New Zealand, Indian, French and later American military formations One of the key figures in this company was Field Marshal Erwin Rommel, who led the German Afrika Korps His tactics and strategy in battles often brought success to the Axis in Africa On the other hand, General Bernard Montgomery, commander of the British 8th Army
